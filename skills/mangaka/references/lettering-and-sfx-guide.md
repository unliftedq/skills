# Lettering and SFX Guide

Use this reference when choosing a lettering route, writing the lettering bible, and verifying finished pages.

## Lettering Bible Fields

Lock these before page generation:

- language/script
- reading direction
- dialogue font or hand-lettering traits
- balloon shape and stroke
- balloon tail style
- caption box style
- emphasis style: bold, larger text, jitter, underline, or balloon shape change
- whisper/shout/thought/electronic voice treatment
- minimum text size and contrast rule
- placement rule for avoiding faces, hands, action lines, and focal points

## Balloon Types

- **Dialogue**: clean oval or rounded balloon with clear tail to speaker.
- **Thought/internal monologue**: cloud, soft tail, caption-like box, or style chosen in bible.
- **Caption/narration**: rectangular or themed box, consistent placement.
- **Shout**: jagged or thicker outline, used sparingly.
- **Whisper**: lighter stroke, smaller type, dashed or soft edge.
- **Electronic/phone/radio**: squared or filtered style, consistent across pages.

## SFX Rules

SFX can be lettering, art, or both. For each recurring SFX type, define:

- source of sound
- text language and whether translation appears
- size and shape
- fill, stroke, shadow, or tone treatment
- relationship to action lines and panel borders
- whether it may cross panel gutters or bleed

Use SFX to clarify action and rhythm. Do not let SFX hide the acting or confuse reading order.

## Text Volume

Keep dialogue short. A practical default:

- 1-2 balloons per panel for action or comedy.
- 2-3 short balloons for drama or exposition.
- Avoid walls of text unless the page design is explicitly built around narration.

If a line is too long for the balloon, rewrite the line or split the panel.

## Art-First Page Requirements

When the selected route is art-first, every generated page should include:

- empty balloons and caption boxes in final positions
- clear balloon tails pointing to the intended speaker
- reserved SFX shapes or spaces when SFX will be added later
- no random placeholder text
- enough clean negative space for final lettering

Save a text map for each page in the prompt packet or in `lettering/<page-number>.md`.

## Verification

- [ ] Text is correctly spelled.
- [ ] Speaker assignment is unambiguous.
- [ ] Balloons follow the selected reading direction.
- [ ] Text does not cover expressions, hands, key props, or action impacts.
- [ ] SFX supports the action and does not clutter the panel.
- [ ] Multilingual text and translations follow the chosen policy.
